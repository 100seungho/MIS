My favorite movie is Tazza. It would be a lie to say that I've seen it dozens of times, but I think I've seen it at least 10 There are many reasons why I like Tazza, but I'll choose a few.

üë∫ Attractive characters

First of all, regardless of the main character and supporting role, the depth of the actors' understanding and acting is very outstanding. Goni (Cho Seung-woo), who dreams of gaining status through gambling, Pyeonggyeong-jang (Baek Yoon-sik), who tells young Gon what to run for a long time through gambling, and Jung Ma-dam (Kim Hye-soo), who represents the dark side of gambling, are characters that clearly show people's desires. Each actor's performance is great, but also each character has a symbolic meaning too.

üé∫ OST and background music

Secondly, it's great OST and background music.

The music used properly in each scene heightens the audience's immersion and creates the illusion that I'm on the gambling table. I've watched it so many times that I can even memorize what kind of music it is playing at what time. Haha. 

üé• Camerawork

Lastly, I choose the best visual beauty. The camerawork is amazing rather than a visual beauty.

My favorite part is the part that appears around 3:57 in the above video. This is the scene that comes out after Goni, whose confidence was hitting the sky, met the legendary "Jjakgui" at the house. While playing games without knowing whether it's daytime or night outside, the bright sunlight of broad daylight hits Goni's eyes strongly as he comes out of the narrow, dark aisle. Goni looks up at the sky, which intentionally increases the exposure value of the camera, giving the audience a glare. With the screen getting brighter little by little, Goni sucking up cigarettes among busy people and lonely background music, Goni's feelings of how small he is in the gambling scene and how newbie is are delivered to me desperately.

Of course, Tazza is an entertainment movie. It's not an artistic film that exposes the pain of society or overflows with new attempts. However, if the entertainment of the movie is so high, I think it definitely did more than it should. Is there a movie that is on the lips of many people even though it's been as long since it's been released as Tazza? By my standards, Tazza is the best movie in that respect.
